# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone who wants a direct, useful review of their resume before applying, sharing it with a recruiter, or asking another person for feedback. They may not know resume conventions deeply, but they want concrete issues they can act on quickly.

## Product Purpose

Resume Reviewer lets a user upload or paste resume text and receive critical feedback by section and line. Success means the user can see what is weak, why it is weak, and what to rewrite without sending the resume to a server or creating an account.

## Brand Personality

Direct, exacting, practical. The product should feel like a sharp editor sitting beside the user: candid about weak wording, allergic to filler, and focused on making the resume more competitive.

## Anti-references

Avoid soft motivational coaching, generic AI chatbot framing, decorative SaaS landing-page patterns, inflated scoring theatrics, and anything that implies resume data is being stored or uploaded when it is not.

## Design Principles

- Make privacy visible and literal: the resume stays in browser memory and disappears when the tab closes.
- Lead with the work surface, not persuasion.
- Give feedback at the smallest useful unit: section, line, and specific rewrite direction.
- Be critical without being vague; every issue should explain the concrete weakness.
- Keep the interface dense enough for repeated editing but calm enough for a stressful job-search task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-operable controls. Do not rely on color alone for severity. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Keep all resume content local to the browser session and avoid persistent storage.
